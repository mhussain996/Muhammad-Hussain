Ertughrul Ghazi – A Ghazi, A Leader, and the Foundation of the Ottoman Empire

Ertughrul Ghazi (according to some traditions, from 1191-1198 to 1280-1281) was the leader of the Kayı tribe. He was the son of Suleyman Shah and the father of Osman I (Osman Ghazi). His life is not only an example of war and jihad, but also of faith, courage, justice, and fighting against the enemies of Islam. He defended his tribe from Mongol attacks, defeated Rum (Byzantine) and other enemies by joining forces with the Seljuk Sultanate, and made Sögüt his center, which later became the foundation of the Ottoman Empire. His life was not only marked by failures but also by many difficulties, pitfalls and shortcomings, but his courage always prevailed.



His Great Deeds – Defeating Enemies. Ertuğrul was a "Ghazi" – a warrior who fought against the enemies of Islam. The greatest feats of his life were:

War against the Mongols

The Mongols were attacking Anatolia. Ertuğrul fought them alongside the Seljuk army. According to some traditions and legends, he defeated a large Mongol army with a small army (in one story, 10,000 Mongols with 300 Alps). He protected the Muslim areas of Anatolia from Mongol attacks and kept the tribe safe. This was his greatest success, as the Mongols were the most feared enemy in the world at that time.



Against the Crusaders/Templars,

Crusaders (Salebi army) and Templar Knights were coming from Europe and trying to conquer Muslim territories. Ertuğrul resisted their attacks, stormed their castles, and defeated them. He fought directly against the Templar Knights and thwarted their plans. This was an important part of his life, in which he defended Islam and prevented the Christian army from gaining a foothold in Anatolia. Ghazwa against the Byzantine Empire The Byzantine Empire was exerting pressure on Muslim areas. Ertuğrul, starting with Söğüt, raided their areas, capturing fortresses like Karacahisar and seizing vast tracts of Muslim land. This was his daily jihad.
